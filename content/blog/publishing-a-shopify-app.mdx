---
title: "Lessons from publishing a public Shopify app"
date: "2026-06-01"
excerpt: "What I learned architecting and shipping a public Shopify app to the App Store, from review requirements to API design."
---

Publishing a public app on the Shopify App Store is a different discipline than
building a private integration for a single client. The review process pushes
you toward stricter API usage, clearer OAuth flows, and more defensive error
handling than most internal tools ever need.

A few things that made the biggest difference:

- **Respecting API rate limits from day one.** Building retry/backoff logic
  early avoided painful rewrites later.
- **Treating uninstall and re-install as first-class flows.** Merchants churn
  in and out — webhooks need to handle that gracefully.
- **Keeping the private app codebase and the public app codebase cleanly
  separated**, even though they shared a lot of business logic.

Shipping the app to the App Store while still maintaining private apps for
enterprise clients meant designing the core logic to be configuration-driven
rather than hardcoded per merchant.

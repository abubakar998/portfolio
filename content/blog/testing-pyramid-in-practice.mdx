---
title: "The testing pyramid, in practice"
date: "2026-04-15"
excerpt: "Notes from automating smoke, regression, and end-to-end suites with Playwright and Cypress on a real production LMS."
---

Working on test automation for a production Learning Management System taught
me that the textbook testing pyramid is a starting point, not a rulebook.

On the Absorb LMS project, the highest-leverage tests weren't always the
lowest-level ones:

- **Smoke suites** caught deployment-breaking issues before they reached QA,
  and needed to run in minutes, not hours.
- **Regression suites** carried the most business risk coverage, so investing
  in stable selectors and retry-safe assertions paid off far more than raw
  test count.
- **End-to-end suites** were reserved for the handful of flows that actually
  span multiple systems — trying to E2E-test everything just made the suite
  slow and flaky.

Playwright and Cypress each had a role: Playwright's multi-browser support was
valuable for cross-browser regression, while Cypress's debugging experience
made it faster to iterate on new UI test cases.
